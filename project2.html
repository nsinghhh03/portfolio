Food Insecurity in
New York Counties

Organization:

Boston University

Role:

Independent Research

Year:

2023

See Full Report

The project investigates food insecurity across New York counties by analyzing publicly available datasets. Using advanced geospatial techniques, it identifies patterns in food access disparities and highlights the influence of socioeconomic and demographic factors. Insights aim to inform strategies for reducing food insecurity and improving resource allocation.

Objective:

To explore the geographical and demographic factors contributing to food insecurity in New York counties, uncovering disparities in food access and their correlation with socioeconomic indicators.

Approach:

The analysis integrated geospatial mapping and demographic data to assess food insecurity across New York counties. Using Rust programming for efficient data processing, the project mapped key variables such as food access, income levels, and population density. Graph analysis techniques were applied to identify correlations between food deserts and vulnerable communities, offering actionable insights into addressing food insecurity.
