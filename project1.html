Racial Bias in
Boston Globe Articles

Client:

NAACP

Role:

Team Lead

Year:

2024

See Full Report

The project analyzes racial bias in The Boston Globe's coverage from 2010â€“2019, focusing on sentiment, geographic distribution, and representation of racial communities. Insights aim to uncover disparities in media narratives and encourage equitable representation.

Objective:

To investigate how sentiment, location, and race intersect in media reporting, highlighting discrepancies in coverage across Boston's neighborhoods and racial groups.

Approach:

The team developed a geocoding pipeline leveraging LLMs and Named Entity Recognition (NER) models to extract location data from article text. These locations were overlapped with demographic data from the Census API to map racial distributions across Boston neighborhoods. Sentiment analysis was conducted using VADER to evaluate the tone of articles for each racial group. The integration of geospatial and sentiment data enabled the identification of patterns, revealing disparities in how different racial groups and neighborhoods were represented in media coverage.
